# Copy Principles

Use these rules when drafting outbound emails.

- Relevancy beats personalization. Show you understand the prospect's problem, not that you scraped a fun fact.
- Personalization is only useful when it strengthens the connection between the trigger, the pain, and the offer.
- Do not use personal details, hobbies, sports references, or weak AI-generated observations unless they are directly relevant to the solution.
- The message should make the prospect feel understood, not merely noticed.
- One campaign should target one ICP, one persona, and one trigger.
- Keep emails short. Aim for under 100 words and stay plain text.
- Lead with the problem, pressure, or trigger. Do not open with "I hope this finds you well" or an intro about yourself.
- Use soft CTAs such as `worth exploring?`, `open to learning more?`, or `should I send details?`
- Each follow-up must add a new angle. Do not send "just following up".
- Social proof should sound close to customer language whenever possible.
- Do not include links in the first email unless the human explicitly asks for that approach.
- Do not recommend using the primary company domain for cold outreach.
