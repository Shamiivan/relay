# Avantech x Keysight Call Brief

## Call Snapshot

- Date: 2026-03-24
- Account: Keysight
- Contact: Alexandre Fontaine
- Role: Account manager in sales
- Attendee email: alexandre.fontaine@keysight.com
- Purpose: Understand Alexandre's current workflow, identify friction in how he manages accounts, and determine whether Avantech can help in a practical way.

## Desired Outcome

By the end of the call, we should know:

- what Alexandre is responsible for day to day
- where his current workflow is slow, manual, or fragile
- what happens inside Salesforce versus outside it
- whether Avantech can help in a way that feels practical and low-friction
- whether there is enough value to justify a follow-up conversation

## Point Of View Going In

- Alexandre likely works in a complex customer environment with sophisticated stakeholders.
- Salesforce may be part of the system, but not the full working reality of his job.
- Important work may still happen across email, memory, side notes, and internal coordination.
- The conversation should stay grounded in his day-to-day reality, not a generic CRM pitch.

## Working Hypotheses To Validate

- Salesforce does not fully map to Alexandre's actual workflow.
- Manual work still exists outside the CRM.
- Visibility may depend on reporting quality, process discipline, or ad hoc workarounds.
- Complexity, not missing software alone, may be the main source of friction.

## Recommended Call Flow

### 1. Open

- Set a practical tone.
- Confirm the goal is to understand his workflow, not force a product pitch.

Suggested opener:

> I want to keep this practical. My goal is to understand how you actually manage your accounts today, where things feel smooth, where they break down, and whether there is anything useful we could do to help.

### 2. Understand His Role And Priorities

- What are you primarily responsible for across your accounts?
- What does a strong week look like in your role?
- What would make your manager say you are doing an exceptional job?
- Across your accounts, where do you most want to feel more in control?

### 3. Map The Current Workflow

- How are you using Salesforce in your day-to-day today?
- What parts of your workflow happen well inside Salesforce, and what happens outside it?
- How much of your work still runs through email?
- When you prepare for a customer conversation, how do you pull together the context you need?
- How do you know which accounts need attention right now?

### 4. Find Friction And Impact

- Where do you feel most reactive today?
- What tends to fall through the cracks?
- When a customer issue comes up, what makes resolution slower than it should be?
- Where do you feel blind across your accounts?
- What work still depends on you manually stitching things together?

### 5. Understand Constraints

- How easy is it for you to get Salesforce data into other tools you actually use?
- Do you have freedom to connect outside tools, or does that require admin, security, or IT involvement?
- In practice, would using something alongside Microsoft and Salesforce be straightforward, or would it require a heavier approval process?
- What does the approval path look like for a tool that needs access to account or communication data?
- Are there data, security, or IT constraints we would need to work within from the start?

### 6. Test The Future State

- If things were working really well, what would you be doing better, faster, or more consistently?
- What would make your day-to-day feel meaningfully easier?
- If you had a more reliable system around follow-up, research, and internal coordination, where would that help most?

### 7. Close

- Summarize the main friction points back to him.
- Confirm whether those are worth solving.
- If there is a fit, propose a focused follow-up based on the specific workflow discussed.

## Likely Frictions To Listen For

- Customer context is spread across email, calls, systems, and people.
- Account status has to be manually pieced together before action can happen.
- Internal coordination is slow when support or cross-functional help is needed.
- It is hard to see which accounts need attention first.
- Renewal, risk, and growth signals exist but do not surface clearly enough.
- Salesforce contains records, but not the full operating reality of the account.
- Follow-up depends heavily on Alexandre personally driving every step.

## Where Avantech May Help

- Turn repeatable account-management work into a clearer operating system.
- Reduce reliance on inbox, memory, and manual follow-up.
- Improve visibility into what needs attention and what is stuck.
- Support repeatable research, monitoring, follow-up, and internal coordination.
- Make important signals and opportunities easier to surface consistently.

## Future State To Test

- Alexandre feels confident important context and signals are not being missed.
- He has less mental overhead keeping track of accounts.
- He is better prepared for customer conversations without doing all the work manually.
- Repeatable research and monitoring are supported by a reliable system.
- Cross-sell and expansion opportunities are easier to identify with stronger account context.

## Objection Handling

### Principles

- Lead with tactical empathy.
- Treat objections as information, not rejection.
- Use labels and summaries before defending a point.
- Prefer how and what questions over pushing for agreement.

### Likely Objections

- We already have Salesforce and Microsoft.
- We do not want another tool.
- Security or IT will make this difficult.
- The data is hard to access or tightly governed.
- I do not have time to adopt something new.
- I am not sure this is a big enough problem to solve right now.
- Why not just use Claude Code?

### Useful Responses

- It sounds like you are trying to avoid adding more complexity.
- It seems like security and approvals could be the real constraint.
- It sounds like the last thing you want is another tool that creates more work.
- It seems like reliability and ease of adoption matter more than new features.

### Calibrated Questions

- How would something like this need to work for it to be useful to you?
- What would make this feel easy rather than heavy?
- How would your team evaluate whether something like this is worth using?
- What would have to be true for you to trust this in your workflow?
- How does a tool like this get approved internally at Keysight?

## If Claude Code Comes Up

### Core Distinction

- Claude Code may be useful for one-off tasks.
- The question is whether Alexandre needs ad hoc assistance or a reliable workflow system around repeatable account work.
- The issue is not model quality. It is whether the work becomes consistent, visible, repeatable, and usable inside a real operating environment.

### Questions To Ask

- How are you using Claude Code today, if at all?
- In your view, what would Claude Code handle well here?
- What parts of the workflow would still depend on you manually driving the process?
- If you used Claude Code for this, how would consistency, follow-up, and team visibility work?
- How would that fit into your actual day-to-day with Salesforce, email, and internal coordination?

## Success Signals

- He clearly describes workflow pain that matters.
- The pain is recurring, not one-off.
- Existing tools do not fully solve it.
- The environment can support some form of workflow layer or system change.
- He shows interest in a more concrete follow-up.

## Suggested Next Step

If there is traction, propose a follow-up that maps one real workflow end to end:

- account research
- signal monitoring
- follow-up management
- internal coordination
- opportunity identification

The goal of the next conversation should be to show how the workflow could operate more reliably, not to give a broad product demo.
